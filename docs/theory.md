# Theory

## 1. Electric Field

The electric field generated by a point charge is given by Coulomb’s law:

$$
\mathbf{E}(\mathbf{r}) = k q \frac{\mathbf{r} - \mathbf{r}_i}{|\mathbf{r} - \mathbf{r}_i|^3}
$$

where:

* $q$ is the charge
* $k$ is Coulomb's constant
* $\mathbf{r}_i$ is the position of the charge
* $\mathbf{r}$ is the observation point

For multiple charges, the total electric field is obtained using the superposition principle:

$$
\mathbf{E}_{total} = \sum_i \mathbf{E}_i
$$

---

## 2. Electric Potential

The electric potential of a point charge is defined as:

$$
V(\mathbf{r}) = \frac{k q}{r}
$$

For a system of charges:

$$
V_{total} = \sum_i \frac{k q_i}{r_i}
$$

The electric field is related to the potential through:

$$
\mathbf{E} = -\nabla V
$$

---

## 3. Regularization of Singularities

At $\mathbf{r} = \mathbf{r}_i$ the electric field becomes singular.
To avoid numerical divergence, a small parameter $\epsilon$ is introduced:

$$
r = \sqrt{dx^2 + dy^2 + \epsilon^2}
$$

This ensures numerical stability near charge locations while preserving the overall field structure.

---

## 4. Particle Dynamics

A charged particle in an electric field experiences a force:

$$
\mathbf{F} = q \mathbf{E}
$$

Using Newton's second law:

$$
\mathbf{a} = \frac{\mathbf{F}}{m} = \frac{q}{m} \mathbf{E}
$$

The particle state is defined as:

$$
(x, y, v_x, v_y)
$$

which evolves over time according to the applied force.

---

## 5. Numerical Integration

The system of equations is:

$$
\frac{d\mathbf{y}}{dt} = f(t, \mathbf{y})
$$

### Runge–Kutta 4 (RK4)

RK4 approximates the solution using four intermediate evaluations:

$$
k_1 = f(t_n, y_n)
$$

$$
k_2 = f(t_n + \tfrac{dt}{2}, y_n + \tfrac{dt}{2}k_1)
$$

$$
k_3 = f(t_n + \tfrac{dt}{2}, y_n + \tfrac{dt}{2}k_2)
$$

$$
k_4 = f(t_n + dt, y_n + dt k_3)
$$

Update rule:

$$
y_{n+1} = y_n + \frac{dt}{6}(k_1 + 2k_2 + 2k_3 + k_4)
$$

RK4 provides high accuracy but does not guarantee energy conservation.

---

### Velocity Verlet

Velocity Verlet is commonly used in physics simulations due to better energy stability:

$$
x_{n+1} = x_n + v_n dt + \frac{1}{2} a_n dt^2
$$

$$
v_{n+1} = v_n + \frac{1}{2}(a_n + a_{n+1}) dt
$$

---

### Boris Method

For electromagnetic simulations, the Boris algorithm is used.
It is a symplectic method that preserves energy and provides stable trajectories for charged particles in electromagnetic fields.

---

## 6. Energy Consideration

The total energy of the system consists of:

* Kinetic energy:

$$
K = \frac{1}{2} m v^2
$$

* Electric potential energy:

$$
U = qV
$$

Total energy:

$$
E = K + U
$$

Energy conservation is used to evaluate the accuracy of numerical integrators.

---

## 7. Summary

This simulation combines:

* Electrostatic field modeling (Coulomb's law)
* Numerical integration methods (RK4, Verlet, Boris)
* Energy analysis for evaluating numerical stability

to study charged particle dynamics in a controlled computational environment.
